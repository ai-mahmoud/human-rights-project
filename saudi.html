2-Saudi Arabia

  • - Human Rights in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia Human rights in Saudi Arabia are based on Islamic Sharia and the Basic Law of Governance. The state works to strengthen these rights through Vision 2030, local regulations, and policies that emphasize fundamental rights such as the right to life, justice, equality, security, work, education, health, and social welfare. The government also focuses on protecting vulnerable groups, including children, women, the elderly, and persons with disabilities.

    • 1. Legal and Sharia Framework:

  • • Islamic Sharia serves as the primary foundation for preserving human rights.
  • • The Basic Law of Governance emphasizes principles of justice, consultation (Shura), and equality.
  • • Saudi Arabia has enacted several laws to protect individual rights, including: The Child Protection Law The Anti-Trafficking in Persons Law The Elderly Care Law

    • 2. Fundamental Rights:

  • • Right to Life and Security: The state ensures protection of. life and security for all citizens. residents.
  • • Equality and Justice: The government is committed. to guaranteeing rights without discrimination.
  • • Right to Work and Education: Vision 2030 aims to empower. women in the labor market. Education and training. opportunities are provided to develop skills for all.
  • • Right to Health and Social Care: Enhancing the quality of healthcare services Strengthening social welfare programs under Vision 2030

    • 3. Rights of Special Groups:

  • • Children: The state protects children. through dedicated laws and programs.
  • • Women: Efforts are made to empower women in the workforce. Enhancing their participation. public and political life.
  • • Elderly: Ensuring their right to dignified life and appropriate services through the Elderly Care Law.
  • • Persons with Disabilities: The state provides prevention, care, and rehabilitation services to support their needs.
