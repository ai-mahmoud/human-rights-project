Human rights in the world

1-EGYPT

  • - The Egyptian state focuses on human rights through the National Human Rights Strategy (2021–2026), which aims to promote and protect all rights and freedoms within a framework of equality and the rule of law. The strategy is based on four main pillars

    • • Main Pillars the Egyptian State Focuses On:

  • 1.Civil and Political Rights: This pillar includes strengthening the right to life and physical integrity, personal freedom, guarantees of fair trial, freedom of expression, freedom of association, freedom of religion and belief, and the right to privacy.
  • 2.Economic, Social, and Cultural Rights: This aspect focuses on ensuring the right to health, education, work, social security, food, access to safe drinking water, adequate housing, and cultural rights.
  • 3.Rights of Priority Groups: The strategy gives special attention to the rights of women, children, persons with disabilities, youth, and the elderly, to ensure their full integration into society and their access to equal opportunities.
  • 4.Human Rights Education and Capacity Building: This pillar aims to promote awareness of human rights values and principles, and to provide specialized training for workers across various state sectors, including security and judicial institutions, to ensure compliance with national and international standards.

    • -Other Key Highlights:

  • • Constitutional and Legal Framework: The Egyptian Constitution affirms fundamental human rights principles, such as non-discrimination, equality, equal opportunities, and the independence of the judiciary as essential guarantees for the protection of rights.
  • • National Institutions: Institutions such as the Permanent Supreme Committee for Human Rights and the National Council for Human Rights work to follow up on the implementation of the strategy, offer guidance, and prepare periodic reports on the state's efforts.
  • • International Cooperation: Egypt actively participates in the international human rights system, including its membership in the United Nations Human Rights Council, and submits regular reports on its international commitments.
  • • The state affirms that these efforts come as part of a comprehensive development process aimed at building a “New Republic” worthy of the dignity of every Egyptian citizen
