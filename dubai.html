3-Dubai

  • • Human rights in Dubai are reflected within the broader framework of the United Arab Emirates through constitutional guarantees and essential social and economic rights, such as the right to education and healthcare, in addition to protecting vulnerable groups including women, children, and people of determination. The state focuses on the principles of the rule of law, ensuring fair trials, promoting values of tolerance, protecting migrant workers, and combating discrimination and human trafficking.

    • 1. Key Points of Human Rights in Dubai

  • • Basic Rights: The constitution guarantees. citizens and residents the right to life, security, freedom of expression and belief, and prohibits torture and degrading treatment.
  • • Social and Economic Rights: Constitutional rights include education, housing, and healthcare for all citizens and residents, as well as a social support network for the most vulnerable groups.
  • • Rights of Migrant Workers: The state is committed to. protecting the rights of migrant workers by ensuring adequate housing, preventing passport confiscation, and providing confidential mechanisms for reporting labor disputes.
  • • Rule of Law: The constitution ensures the. independence of the judiciary, the right of the accused to a fair trial, and the right to legal representation.
  • • Vulnerable Groups: The constitution pays special attention to the rights of women, children, the elderly, and people of determination (persons with disabilities).
  • • Equality andNon-Discrimination: Legislation aims to protect against discrimination and promote gender equality, including modern laws such as equal pay regulations.
  • • Tolerance and Coexistence: The state seeks to promote values of tolerance and coexistence among more than 200 nationalities living within its borders.
  • • Combating Human Trafficking: The state implements. measures to combat human trafficking.
